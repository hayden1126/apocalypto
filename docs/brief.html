Field Research Brief · updated 2026-07-08 · Prototyping — steps 1–2 measured

Navigation for when the grid is gone

A technology reality-check for an offline, GPS-degraded map + phone-to-phone hazard-sharing app built for disaster and civil-unrest zones. Four capability tracks, researched and adversarially verified against the evidence.

Threat driversNatural disaster + civil unrest
PlatformPhone-first + optional LoRa/solar
TracksPositioning · Vision · Mesh · Power
Evidence105 agents · 3 runs · 56 claims verified
Bottom line

Buildable — but only if you invert the pitch.

The defensible product is an offline map with a self-contained dead-reckoning position and a phone-to-phone hazard layer. The seductive premise, point your camera and recognize the ruined street from pre-disaster photos, is not supported by the evidence and should be cut from v1.

The single biggest risk is adversarial and orthogonal to sensor quality: in the unrest/war model GNSS is routinely jammed and spoofed, and no serverless mesh is Sybil-proof (every audited app, notably Bridgefy, was fully broken). Better sensors fix neither. Only structure does, make dead reckoning the trusted backbone, never trust a lone GNSS fix, and gate every hazard report behind signing, corroboration, and expiry.

Measured since publish · 2026-07-08

Two prototype steps now test the positioning spine on real data. Step 1 (open IMU benchmark): gyro-only dead reckoning holds ~3% of distance and periodic trusted-GNSS re-anchoring bounds the drift to street level, while the magnetometer only hurts. Step 2 (first real-phone walk — iPhone, ~595 m, 3 loops): ~6.5% drift, still bounded, but a phone's faster heading drift needs a trusted fix every ~15–20 s, not 30. Converged phone GPS was ~1–2 m (the 14 m iOS self-reports is pessimistic). The multi-kilometer hold is the one open question.

01

Track assessment

Track AUsable · needs resets

GPS-denied positioning

A drift-limited problem. Step counting is solved (~99.5%); heading is the binding constraint, and no validated multi-kilometer phone-only benchmark exists anywhere.

Shippable now

IMU dead reckoning snapped to a cached OSM graph (map-matching keeps the right street; it does not erase drift), with periodic trusted-GNSS resets that bound it.

Frontier

Foot-mounted ZUPT pod (best accessory bet, buildable now); UWB phone-to-phone, ~2–4 yrs.

FAILSCompass 30–90° off in rubble and steel — exactly this app's terrain — plus jamming/spoofing.

Track BDe-scoped

Camera localization

The pillar the evidence does not support. All commercial VPS (ARCore Geospatial, Lightship) is cloud-only, useless in the exact outage this targets.

Shippable now

Only coarse manual tools: skyline-to-terrain matching, human-in-the-loop landmark confirmation.

Frontier

Recognizing collapsed streets: no visible inflection in 2020–2025. Treat as 5–10 yrs, low confidence.

FAILSNight (85%→44%), smoke (a cliff), and moved buildings (~0%, no published accuracy). DARPA SubT was won on LiDAR, not cameras.

Track CWorks · trust unsolved

Mesh + hazard trust

BLE is the only cross-platform baseline (10–100 m/hop), hobbled by iOS/Android background limits. A LoRa companion buys an order of magnitude of range.

Shippable now

BLE hazard beacons + a spatial CRDT, gated by signed, N≥3-corroborated, TTL-expiring reports.

Frontier

Purpose-built geohash delta-CRDT and crowdsourced jamming detection, ~1–2 yrs.

FAILSSparse density starves the mesh; data poisoning has no serverless arbiter — raise Sybil cost, never eliminate it.

Track DSolved · GNSS-constrained

Power, hardware & EMP

The decisive finding reorders priorities: the screen and camera/CV dominate draw, not GNSS or BLE. Autonomy is a solved problem if you duty-cycle.

Shippable now

3–5 days on a 20,000 mAh bank; effectively indefinite with a 10–20 W folding panel. Dark vector map by default.

Frontier

EMP: a powered-off, uncabled phone is least-vulnerable, but survival is inferred, never measured.

FAILSOffline spoof-detection can't catch matched-power GNSS spoofing (its key check needs connectivity). EMP stays Faraday-bag insurance.

02

The positioning stack

Position is a distribution, not a dot. Feed it cheap continuous motion; spend scarce, untrusted absolute fixes only to shrink the uncertainty cone. When a layer fails, drop to the next, and the cone visibly widens.

L0

Trusted GNSS reset

Raw GNSS, anomaly-gated (drop mock-provider / C-N0 anomalies), duty-cycled to one fix per ~15–30 s (a phone's faster heading drift needs the tighter end — measured step 2). In the unrest profile, downgraded to advisory-only. Collapses the cone when it passes.

Absolute · scarce
↓ on loss / spoof
L1

IMU pedestrian dead reckoning

Step detection + EKF heading fusion (ships today as Android ROTATION_VECTOR / iOS CoreMotion). Always-on, low power, survives jamming, smoke, and darkness. Grows the cone.

Continuous backbone
↓ bound the drift
L2

Map-matching to cached OSM

On-device HMM/Viterbi snaps the drifting trace onto plausible graph edges (a ~5 m graph-offset floor). It disambiguates which street, cutting lateral drift on dense grids, but cannot fix how far along, which needs a GNSS reset. Needs an off-graph state for open-field evacuation.

Which-street disambiguator
↓ when a fix is possible
L3

Coarse re-anchor: vision + peers

Not street recognition. Skyline-to-terrain where a horizon is exposed, human-confirmed landmarks, and peer-shared trusted fixes. Duty-cycled — camera + CV is a multi-watt drain that fails abruptly in smoke and dark.

Degraded · optional
↓ nothing left
◔

Pure PDR + a widening uncertainty cone

The honest UI primitive. The user is never shown a false-confidence dot — the cone is what makes a spoofed or blind environment safe to operate in.

Honest fallback
03

MVP call

◆ Ship in v1
  1. 01Offline vector maps + routing from pre-cut regional packages (low-single-digit GB).
  2. 02Positioning L0–L2: gated GNSS, IMU PDR, map-matching, with an honest uncertainty cone.
  3. 03BLE hazard layer: extended-advertising beacons + spatial-CRDT edits under 237 bytes.
  4. 04The trust gate: signed + N≥3 corroboration + TTL expiry. Market it as corroboration, not attack-proof.
  5. 05Duty-cycling power manager with a dark vector-map default.
  6. 06First-class companion hardware: pair a LoRa node + solar over BLE ($20–50). The real resilience story.
✕ Drop from v1
  • ✕CV auto-recognition of streets. Collapses at night, in smoke, and when buildings have moved.
  • ✕All cloud VPS. Architecturally useless in the exact outage we target.
  • ✕UWB & Wi-Fi RTT. No cross-platform interop; need powered anchors a dead grid lacks.
  • ✕Sub-meter RTK/PPP. Depends on correction streams that need connectivity.
  • ✕Magnetic/radio fingerprinting. Needs a pre-disruption survey damage may invalidate.
  • ✕Any "Sybil-proof / spoof-proof" claim. Keep the mitigations, drop the promise.
04

Capture layer · CV, earned back

Cutting auto-localization didn't kill the camera — it repositioned it. The camera is a mapping tool (record the changed world for the people behind you), never a positioning oracle. Both features reuse the existing signed-and-corroborated trust gate, and every capture-derived re-anchor enters as a low-priority candidate the uncertainty cone can overrule — that boundary is exactly where the rejected self-localization tries to sneak back in.

Feature 3Ship v1 · classical core

Fixed-vantage change detection

Re-occupy a named vantage, ghost-overlay align, human blink/slider review, emit a tiny signed pointer. Self-referential at one named place, so it sidesteps the aliasing trap.

Shippable now

ORB/AKAZE + RANSAC homography align (~0.15–0.3 s), human-confirmed. Cheap, classical, no ML.

Frontier

On-device change proposers (TinyCD-class), ~2–4 yr — still human-confirmed, never autonomous.

FAILSA sparse sampler that cries wolf — illumination/shadow false alarms, and it misses change between duty-cycled revisits. Keep it narrow, optional, human-confirmed.

Feature 1v1 = dumb layer · rebuild deferred

Re-survey / mapping

"Match the new world against itself" into relocalizable geometry is a 2–5 yr frontier. v1 ships the honest version: floating geotagged fresh imagery, dedup, bulk sync, human overlay.

Shippable now

Capture tagged with current pose + uncertainty; 8-byte pHash + thumbnail on the mesh; raw imagery by bulk on contact.

Frontier

On-device SfM / Gaussian-splat rebuild (1–3 yr, iOS-only today); federated crowd-mapping (2–5 yr).

FAILSOne false submap merge (same aliasing) silently misplaces a chunk of survey data later users trust. Gate every weld: RANSAC + corroboration + human-confirm.

▤ What moves where
Rides the mesh · bytes

pHash signature (8 B), descriptor hash, vantage/segment ID, timestamp, change-class, confidence, author signature + TTL, thumbnail hash.

Bulk only · MB–GB

Panoramas (5–25 MB), 4K video (130–360 MB/min), meshes/submaps, CD model weights. Move on physical contact or at a hub.

⇄ How imagery actually moves
  • ▸SoftAP + mDNS + HTTPS (LocalSend-style) — cross-platform, link-limited. The load-bearing default.
  • ▸USB-C cable — 0.48–40 Gbps, RF-immune. The floor that always works.
  • ▸AirDrop / Quick Share — fast (100s of Mbps) but same-OS only. Opportunistic, never depended on.
  • ▸iOS↔Android bulk is still a wall — Wi-Fi Aware is broken today; the Quick Share bridge is Pixel-first, not an SDK.
05

Reach · wilderness & drones

Two probes at the edges of scope. Both earn a place; neither earns the core. Urban/populated disaster stays the design center — each of these is inert-safe and gated behind a trigger (wilderness behind "no peers," drone behind ownership), so the app works fully with neither present.

Coast / islandGO as solo mode · not a disaster app

Wilderness degradation

In the wild you lose the road-graph's which-street correction but gain the best GPS a phone ever sees (~4.9 m open-sky vs 7–13 m urban). The stack reuses cheaply — but the mesh + crowd-hazard core is structurally dead with no population.

Shippable now

Raw-GPS-primary on the same anchor/cone UI + breadcrumb return + compass + offline DEM/topo + manual celestial backstop. Near-zero R&D, mostly reuse.

Frontier

Barometric-DEM terrain matching (~2–3 yr) and skyline-to-DEM re-anchor (~2–4 yr) — both terrain-gated, roadmap only.

FAILSFlat beaches/atolls (<3–5 m relief) kill terrain matching (165 m on the only free DEM); canopy degrades GPS and DEM together. Gate on land-cover, sell as a solo survival instrument, never a shrunk disaster app.

Drone tierOptional gear · gated on ownership

Aerial re-survey · scouting · relay

A real force-multiplier for the ~8% who own a DJI-class drone; never a core dependency. Aerial nadir re-survey is the standout — top-down imagery beats ground capture on aliasing and self-georeferences.

Shippable now

Re-survey (hub-stitched orthomosaic feeds the map), route scouting (drops hazard pins into the mesh), burst LoRa relay (~4–5× line-of-sight range).

Frontier

Phone-native orthomosaic + GPS-denied auto-georef (1–3 yr); multi-hop relay / BVLOS (3–7 yr, regulation-blocked).

FAILSGPS denial — the scenario — attacks the drone's own return-to-home and hover. Video range collapses 10–20× to ~0.5–1 km in the urban canyon; endurance 20–46 min. Optional gear, never load-bearing.

06

Open risks & kill criteria

Primary

GNSS trust is unsolvable offline. Matched-power spoofing returns a false fix that looks valid, and the strongest software check needs connectivity we won't have. Constraint: GNSS is advisory-only in the unrest profile from day one; PDR + map-matching is the trusted backbone.

High

No validated multi-km phone-only positioning benchmark exists. 1° of heading error is ~17.5 m of drift per km, and the compass degrades worst in rubble. Kill/pivot: if map-matched PDR exceeds ~one street width over realistic 1–3 km disaster walks, require the foot-mounted ZUPT accessory. Phase-1 field trial must generate this missing benchmark.

High

Serverless trust can become an attack amplifier. 1,000 Waze Sybils once ran from a single machine; poisoned "safe" roads reroute people into danger. Kill: if corroboration + decay can't keep a fabricated hazard's lifetime low under modeled Sybil attack, degrade to read-only from socially-trusted peers.

Watch

BLE background relay is throttled hard by both mobile OSes, and mesh needs crowd density that a sparse early-disaster zone lacks. Pivot: LoRa-companion-primary — which is why companion hardware is first-class in the MVP, not an add-on.

Watch

Camera CV in combined smoke + rubble + dark shows no near-term path. Already de-scoped. Re-promote only if: a distilled robust model clears ~50% recall under those combined conditions and someone publishes relocalization against a post-collapse reference. Plan as if neither happens.
