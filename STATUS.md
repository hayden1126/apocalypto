## Findings (step 1, 2026-07-07)
- **PDR half PASSES the gate.** Gyro-only PDR (with static-phase gyro-bias removal) holds rmse ~3% of distance on every outdoor track: TEST_02 6.7 m / 231 m, TEST_04 13.2 m / 375 m, TEST_05 18.0 m / 565 m, TEST_06 14.0 m / 465 m. Squarely in the 2-5% regime the literature predicts. Phone-only PDR is viable at street scale for hundreds of metres.
- **The magnetometer HURT.** Naive Madgwick MARG fusion degraded heading badly (rmse 28-137 m) across all tracks; gyro + static-bias-removal wins. Naive mag fusion is not a usable absolute-heading reference; it needs hard gating or a different mechanism.
- **Heading drift is UNBOUNDED without an absolute reference,** so the longest track is the worst. This is the real limiter for the multi-km claim: over km, heading error will exceed street spacing.
- **Map-matching corrects "which street", not "where along it".** Graph/matcher floor is ~5 m (OSM footway-centerline offset from the actual walked path, the pedestrian-graph pitfall quantified). On dense urban (TEST_05) map-matching recovered ~90% of the correct route and cut cross-track error 10.3 -> 7.2 m, but overall rmse only 18 -> 16.5 m because along-track (step-length) error dominates and matching cannot fix it. On open/parking (TEST_06) it was near-neutral. It is NOT a drift-correction oracle: it only disambiguates while drift stays below street spacing.
- **Periodic GNSS re-anchoring BOUNDS the drift, and the required frequency is quantified.** Simulated on the GEOLOC GNSS (`scripts/reanchor_experiment.py`, `out/reanchor_curve.png`): PDR fills the gaps between occasional trusted fixes; a fix every ~30 s (~35 m at walking pace) holds residual RMSE <=5-6 m on every track including urban; every ~15 s reaches ~3-4 m (the raw GNSS noise floor). So the "degraded GPS" premise is workable: continuous GPS is not required, only occasional trusted fixes. Two caveats: a single early anchor is useless (value is in REPEATED anchoring); and each fix must be TRUSTED, so GNSS spoofing (the brief's structural risk) attacks this loop directly.

## Findings (step 2 phone shakedown walk, 2026-07-08)
First real phone walk: iPhone 14 Pro, Sensor Logger 1.60.1 at 100 Hz, ~595 m (3 loops of a ~50 m block, Ma Ling Path, HK), 683 steps, ~8.7 min with 60 s static bookends, a counted 15.3 m / 18-step calibration leg (hydrant -> first lamppost, distance from Google satellite), closed on a fire hydrant. Preceded by two 30 s test clips (indoor) that verified the adapter ingests real iPhone data (columns map by name despite `z,y,x` order; 100 Hz; Accelerometer + Gravity reconstruction gives |a| ~ 9.8) and that GPS is dead indoors by physics. Raw exports are session-scratchpad only (ephemeral, uncommitted: privacy). Verification via `run_phone --k 0.537` + the reanchor metric.
- **Phone PDR drift ~6.5% over ~595 m** (loop closure, GPS-free, k-invariant): 38.6 m end-vs-start on a truly closed loop. ~2x the step-1 Xsens 3%, same order. Loops precess (heading drift), visible as a fanning spiral, not a bias offset.
- **Gyro bias is stable, NOT the culprit.** Opening-vs-closing static yaw-bias drift 0.042 deg/s (~8 deg accumulated heading error over the ~400 s walk = 0.042 deg/s x 403 s / 2, the /2 for a linear ramp after the pipeline removes the opening bias). The 6.5% is gyro random-walk + integration noise, not a fixable single bias. Phone gyro is honestly decent.
- **Honest step length k = 0.537** (median step 0.85 m, a touch long: deliberate pacing) from the counted 15.3 m leg. GPS-calibrated k (0.679) was inflated 1.27x by GPS jitter + an 808 m outlier fix (GPS path 753 m vs true ~595 m). Concrete proof of the protocol rule: never GPS-calibrate k for the headline.
- **Re-anchoring bounds the drift but the phone needs a TIGHTER cadence than the Xsens.** Held-out RMSE at honest k: 15 s -> 4.0 m, 20 s -> 7.1 m, 30 s -> 14.7 m, 45 s -> 24.5 m, 60 s -> 48.7 m. Step-1 Xsens held 5-6 m at 30 s; the phone needs ~15-20 s (a trusted fix every ~20-25 m) to hold street level, because phone HEADING drifts 2x faster (re-anchoring position every 30 s does not fix heading between anchors, and over a ~20 m half-gap a precessing heading throws ~15 m cross-track). This is a PDR-drift penalty, NOT a GPS-quality one (converged GPS was ~1-2 m; see below). The thesis (PDR + periodic trusted re-anchoring) holds on a real phone, with this quantified cadence penalty.
- **GPS is NOT the ceiling (corrected 2026-07-08 after Hayden noted the loop was open-sky, 2-3 storey residential).** iOS self-reported 14.2 m median accuracy, but that is pessimistic: measured directly from the closing 60 s static (55 fixes, phone stationary at the hydrant), ACTUAL precision was std 1.2 m / p95 1.9 m while iOS still reported 8.9 m. Path inflation (753 m GPS vs true 595 m, 1.27x) independently implies ~5 m walking jitter. So converged phone GPS here is ~1-2 m stationary / ~2-5 m walking, well inside the 20-30 m PASS headroom (the "~5 m floor" guard holds). The real GPS issues were (1) COLD START: the opening static caught only 4 scattered fixes (35 m spread) before the receiver locked, so GPS must be warmed to a full lock BEFORE recording; and (2) one 808 m outlier fix, so trusted-fix re-anchoring REQUIRES outlier rejection (also exactly where GNSS spoofing, the brief's structural risk, attacks). The venue was fine; no better venue needed.
- **Map-matching recovered only 44% of edges** (route_edge_overlap 0.444): thin OSM footway coverage on the block and/or drift too large. Check OSM coverage of any multi-km route before walking it.
- **Limits:** n=1 walker/phone, one pose (P1 held-flat), ~595 m (NOT multi-km), GPS not warmed to a full lock at recording start (cold-start garbage in the first ~90 s), single direction (no CW/CCW bracketing), no surveyed checkpoints. First real signal that the phone pipeline works and drift is bounded; not proof. The multi-km hold and the PASS/KILL verdict are still open.

## Findings (gated-magnetometer experiment, 2026-07-08)
Three open-loop heading variants (gyro-only, naive MARG, gated MARG), heading-isolated (GT strides) + open-loop position RMSE, on disturbed tracks TEST_01/03 (indoor office/stairs) and the four clean-outdoor controls; threshold sweep mag_tol {.05,.1,.2} x dip_tol {5,10,20} deg. Heading-isolated RMSE (m):

| track | accept% | gyro | naive | gated(0.10/10deg) | gated(best) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| TEST_01 (disturbed) | 47 | 50.0 | 36.3 | 35.5 | 33.2 |
| TEST_03 (disturbed) | 48 | 2.4 | 35.4 | 16.8 | 6.1 |
| TEST_02 | 51 | 3.7 | 34.1 | 9.8 | 5.1 |
| TEST_04 | 61 | 8.1 | 91.6 | 80.5 | 19.6 |
| TEST_05 | 36 | 17.3 | 104.8 | 14.2 | 8.0 |
| TEST_06 | 34 | 14.8 | 29.5 | 22.4 | 15.5 |

- **Gating always rescues naive MARG** (34-105 m) to far lower error. Confirmed: never fuse mag blind; if mag is used at all, gate it.
- **But it does NOT beat the gyro-only baseline** except where gyro drift is already severe (TEST_01: 50.0 -> 33-35 m; TEST_05 marginal 17.3 -> 14.2 m). Where gyro-only is already good, gating HURTS (TEST_03 2.4 -> 16.8 m, TEST_02 3.7 -> 9.8 m, TEST_04 8.1 -> 80.5 m at a fixed threshold).
- **No universal threshold.** The optimal gate aggressiveness points in OPPOSITE directions by track: TEST_01 (bad gyro drift) wants a loose gate to USE mag; TEST_03 (good gyro) wants a tight gate to REJECT it. You cannot know offline which regime you are in, so there is no deployable fixed setting. Even oracle per-track tuning (gated-best) rarely beats gyro-only on the already-good tracks.
- **Verdict: gating does not make the magnetometer a dependable net-positive over gyro-only for GPS-denied heading.** It fixes naive MARG's catastrophe and helps only once gyro drift is already large, needing unavailable per-environment tuning. The fully-GPS-denied case still needs a foot-mounted ZUPT accessory or accept-drift, not the magnetometer. Strengthens the step-1 mag ruling.
- **Limits:** non-phone Xsens IMU, moderate indoor disturbance (not a rebar-heavy disaster building), heading isolated via GT strides. First signal, not proof.
